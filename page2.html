Favourite Places

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a major tourist attraction and attracts a large number of domestic and foreign visitors. About five million visitors visited Taj Mahal in the financial year 2022-23. A three-tier pricing system is in place, with a significantly lower entrance fee for Indian citizens and more expensive ones for foreigners. The Taj Mahal combines elements of Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. The complex includes a grand mosque, a guest house, and beautiful gardens. The central dome, surrounded by four smaller domes and four slender minarets, is particularly striking.

Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, is the holiest gurdwara of Sikhism, located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India. Built around a man-made pool, it was completed in 1604 by the fifth Sikh Guru, Guru Arjan. The temple complex also houses the world's largest free kitchen, serving meals to thousands of visitors daily.

Mysore Palace

Mysore Palace, also known as Amba Vilas Palace, is a historical palace located in the city of Mysore in Karnataka, India. It is the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore. The palace is illuminated with thousands of bulbs during the annual Dasara festival, attracting tourists from around the world.

Thanjavur Temple

Thanjavur Temple, also known as Brihadeeswarar Temple, is a magnificent Hindu temple located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, India. Built by Raja Raja Chola I between 1003 and 1010 AD, it is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a part of the "Great Living Chola Temples." The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture. Thanjavur Temple is not only a significant religious center but also a testament to the architectural and artistic achievements of the Chola dynasty.
